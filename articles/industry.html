Developing smart factories provides an incredible opportunity for the industrial sector to enter the fourth industrial revolution. Analyzing the vast amount of big data collected from sensors on a factory floor provides real-time visibility of production assets and can provide tools to perform predictive maintenance to minimize downtime.

Using high-tech IoT devices in smart factories leads to higher productivity and better quality. Replacing manual inspection business models with AI-based visual information reduces production errors and saves time and money. With minimal investment, quality control personnel can set up a cloud-connected smartphone to monitor manufacturing processes from wherever they are. By applying machine learning algorithms, manufacturers can detect errors immediately, rather than at an advanced stage when a repair would be more expensive.

Trip to transformation: from a course to the field

Carpenteria meccanica di precisione

Mercato Saraceno (FC)

On the occasion of a collaboration between my DTM course and the SCA company, I went with my lab group to visit the production plant. After an introductory phase on the internal processes of the factory, our challenge was to apply what we had studied in class in practice.

Using a sensor we were to introduce a small digital transformation to help the enterprise manage some of its operations. The puck.js sensor is an intelligent Bluetooth button. With the Espruino JavaScript interpreter on its internal ARM microcontroller, software can be uploaded, debugged and modified wirelessly from any modern computer or phone, making development of embedded devices significantly easier. It has built-in sensors, lights, infrared transmitter, GPIO, and can even control other Bluetooth LE devices.

On that day, we collected data, and it will be our challenge to analyze it and produce a design proposal for the company.
